Understanding the Ongoing War

This independent site shares publicly available facts about the war in Ukraine and directs support to transparent, verified organizations.

The Current Situation (as of April 2026)

russia's illegal full-scale invasion of Ukraine began in February 2022 and continues into its fifth year. Russia currently occupies roughly 20% of Ukrainian territory.

Fighting remains active with slow Russian advances in some areas (particularly Donetsk region) and Ukrainian counteractions in others. Both sides conduct long-range strikes on military and infrastructure targets.

The situation evolves rapidly. Continued humanitarian needs persist across the country.

Sources: Institute for the Study of War (ISW), UN reports. Last updated: April 2026.

Impact on Civilians

Millions of people in Ukraine require humanitarian assistance due to ongoing conflict, displacement, and damage to infrastructure.

Key challenges include frequent air alerts, power outages, damage to homes and essential services, and effects on families and children.

Based on UN OCHA and humanitarian reports (2026).

Ukrainian Culture & Heritage

Ukraine is known for its rich traditions, vibrant folk art, literature, music, and iconic symbols such as the sunflower and embroidered vyshyvanka.

Traditional foods like borscht and varenyky, along with a deep historical and cultural heritage, represent what Ukrainians are defending.

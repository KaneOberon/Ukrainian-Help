Understanding the Ongoing War

This independent site shares publicly available facts about the war in Ukraine and directs support to transparent, verified organizations.

The Current Situation (as of April 2026)

russia's illegal full-scale invasion of Ukraine began in February 2022 and continues into its fifth year. Russia currently occupies roughly 20% of Ukrainian territory.

Fighting remains active with slow Russian advances in some areas (particularly Donetsk region) and Ukrainian counteractions in others, including northern Kharkiv and southern directions. Both sides conduct long-range strikes: Russia launches hundreds of drones and missiles against Ukrainian cities and infrastructure, while Ukraine targets Russian military assets and energy facilities.

The situation evolves rapidly. Recent reports indicate continued drone and missile attacks on civilian areas, with significant humanitarian needs persisting.

Sources: Institute for the Study of War (ISW), UN reports, and other public assessments. Last updated: April 2026.

Impact on Civilians

Over 10.8 million people in Ukraine require humanitarian assistance in 2026, including millions who are internally displaced or affected by ongoing attacks.

Key challenges include:

  • Frequent air alerts and strikes on energy infrastructure causing power outages and heating disruptions, especially in winter.
  • Damage to homes, schools, and hospitals, affecting access to education and healthcare.
  • Displacement: Tens of thousands newly displaced in recent months due to intensified fighting in frontline areas.
  • Civilian casualties from long-range attacks and frontline hostilities.

Humanitarian organizations continue to provide food, shelter, medical aid, and protection support to those most in need.

Based on UN OCHA and humanitarian reports (2026).

Ukrainian Culture & Heritage

Ukraine is known for its rich traditions, including vibrant folk art, literature, and music. Iconic symbols include the sunflower, embroidered vyshyvanka shirts, and traditional foods such as borscht and varenyky (dumplings).

The country has a deep history with diverse regional cultures, world-renowned writers, composers, and a strong commitment to freedom and identity.

This cultural heritage is an important part of what Ukrainians are defending.
